Reinforcement Learning-based Dynamic Difficulty Adjustment Master Thesis

Under Review

Read More

Dynamic Difficulty Adjustment (DDA) is a viable approach to enhance a player's experience in video games. Recently, Reinforcement Learning (RL) methods have been employed for DDA in non-competitive games; nevertheless, they rely solely on discrete state-action space with a small search space. In this paper, we propose a continuous RL-based DDA methodology for a visual working memory (VWM) game to handle the complex search space for the difficulty of memorization. The proposed RL-based DDA tailors game difficulty based on the player's score and game difficulty in the last trial.

Enhanced Screening Accuracy of ASD Toddlers using Multi-modal System

Accepted

Read More

Early screening for autism spectrum disorder (ASD) can significantly improve intervention outcomes. However, acknowledging the diverse nature of ASD and the variety of its symptoms, a multi-modal approach becomes necessary for effective autism screening. In this study, we propose a framework powered by the Robotic Operating System (ROS) to integrate various ASD screening tools as separate modalities seamlessly. With a focus on behavioral data, our preliminary study involves the analysis of three distinct ASD symptom groups using three modalities: the sensorized toy car for assessing repetitive behaviors, the Lightwheel for analyzing sensory input sensitivity, and the Roboparrot for simulating social interaction initiation. By fusing the results of each modality, we achieved enhanced accuracy and reliability in ASD screening, surpassing the individual modality accuracy by more than 10% on the average.
